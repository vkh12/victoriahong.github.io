I enjoy turning complex problems into simple, beautiful, and intuitive designs. When I am not coding, you can find me hosting robotics workshops for high school students, experimenting new low-calorie recipes, or finding a good deal on Crest 3D Whitening toothpaste.

Having grown up in a small town with two immigrant parents, I did not have access to an adequate STEM education system that provided me with resources to extracurricular activities, like robotics club and coding camp. As a direct result, I devote my time to advocating for these specific opportunities for students in my community and State, mentoring a robotics team, and spearheading robotics and coding camps.

By pursuing a computer engineering major and computer science minor at Rutgers University, not only am I following my dream of becoming an engineer but, I can use my skills and experiences to cater to what I enjoy doing the most: building robots and designing websites.
